## The Easiest Way to Find Any Roblox Song ID in 3 Steps (2025 Guide)

Stop trusting random YouTube comments from three years ago. The library changes daily. Here is the modern, manual way to find working IDs.

### Step 1: Navigate to the Creator Marketplace
The standard search bar is useless for audio. Instead, go to the **Roblox Website** on your browser (PC is best). Click on the "Create" tab, then look for "Store" or "Creator Marketplace." Once there, select **"Audio"** from the dropdown menu and ensure you filter by **"Music"** (not Sound Effects).

### Step 2: Search and Verify the Track
Type the song name or artist you are looking for. **Pro Tip:** If the original song is copyrighted (like Drake or Taylor Swift), search for "remix," "slowed," or "cover" versions. Roblox moderation often lets these slide. Click on the audio file to preview it and make sure it’s actually the song and not just static noise.

### Step 3: Extract the ID from the URL
This is the magic step. Look at the URL bar at the top of your browser. It will look something like this:
`roblox.com/library/1234567890/Song-Name`
The **long string of numbers** in the middle (e.g., `1234567890`) is your **Roblox ID Code**. Copy just those numbers, and paste them into your in-game boombox.

## FAQ Section

**Q: Why do some IDs I find say "Content Deleted"?**
**A:** This happens when the audio violates Roblox's Terms of Service or copyright laws. If you find an ID and it doesn't work in-game, the audio asset has likely been purged by moderation. You'll need to search for a new version of the song.

**Q: Can I upload my own music if I can't find it?**
**A:** Yes! You can upload audio files via the Creator Dashboard. However, it costs a small fee (in Robux) depending on the file length, and there are monthly upload limits. Plus, you must own the rights or risk a copyright strike.

**Q: Do these IDs work in every Roblox game?**
**A:** Most of them, yes. However, some developers restrict their games to only play royalty-free music approved by Roblox to avoid copyright issues. If your boombox works but no sound comes out, the game might have "Copyrighted Audio" disabled.
